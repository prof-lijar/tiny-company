# TOPIK Content Guide

This document defines the content structure and requirements for the TOPIK Learning Assistant, ensuring that all practice materials align with the official TOPIK II (Levels 3-6) standards.

## 1. TOPIK Level Descriptions

| Level | Proficiency | Key Capabilities | Target Content Focus |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Level 3** | Intermediate Low | Can carry out transactions in public spaces and maintain social relationships. Understands basic characteristics of Korean. | Daily life in public, basic social interactions, standard polite speech. |
| **Level 4** | Intermediate High | Comprehends news articles, general social issues, and abstract topics with accuracy and fluency. Understands representative aspects of Korean culture. | Social issues, news, common idioms, abstract concepts, formal written style. |
| **Level 5** | Advanced Low | High fluency in professional research or work. Can discuss unfamiliar topics in politics, economics, and other fields. | Professional discourse, politics, economics, specialized vocabulary. |
| **Level 6** | Advanced High | Absolutely fluent for professional research or work. Capacity to understand and express oneself without problem. | Academic papers, complex literary texts, nuanced professional communication. |

## 2. Vocabulary Structure

Vocabulary should be categorized by level and theme.

### Estimated Vocabulary Counts (Cumulative)
- **Level 3**: ~3,000 - 4,000 words
- **Level 4**: ~5,000 - 7,000 words
- **Level 5**: ~8,000 - 10,000 words
- **Level 6**: 12,000+ words

### Vocabulary Categories
- **Daily/Social**: Shopping, health, travel, relationships (Level 3-4)
- **Academic/Formal**: Education, science, history, sociology (Level 4-6)
- **Professional/Specialized**: Economics, politics, law, medicine (Level 5-6)
- **Idioms & Proverbs**: Common Korean idioms (Saja-seong-eo) and proverbs (Level 4-6)

## 3. Grammar Pattern Categories

Grammar should be organized by function and level of complexity.

### Category 1: Sentence Connectors (Linking)
- **Contrast**: -지만, -는데, -음에도 불구하고
- **Cause/Effect**: -아서/어서, -기 때문에, -느라고, -기에
- **Condition**: -면, -아야/어야, -는 한
- **Sequence/Simultaneity**: -으면서, -자마자, -고 나서

### Category 2: Sentence Endings (Mood/Attitude)
- **Formal/Written**: -ㄴ/는다 (Plain form), -습니다 (Formal)
- **Polite/Spoken**: -아요/어요
- **Speculation/Probability**: -을 것 같다, -나 보다, -을 리가 없다
- **Intent/Will**: -으려고 하다, -고자 하다, -기로 하다

### Category 3: Advanced Structures (Level 5-6)
- **Double Negatives/Emphasis**: -지 않을 수 없다
- **Complex Modifiers**: -ㄴ/은/는 채로, -다가는
- **Formal Honorifics**: Specialized professional honorifics

## 4. Reading Passage Types & Difficulty Markers

Reading materials must simulate the TOPIK II format.

| Passage Type | Level | Characteristics | Difficulty Markers |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Short Ads/Notices** | 3-4 | Practical information, schedules, requirements. | Understanding key details, identifying purpose. |
| **Short Essays/Blogs** | 3-4 | Personal opinions, everyday experiences. | Identifying the main idea, author's sentiment. |
| **News Articles** | 4-5 | Factual reporting, social issues, trends. | Synthesizing information, identifying core arguments. |
| **Academic/Opinion** | 5-6 | Logical arguments, specialized theories, research. | Complex sentence structures, high-level academic vocab. |
| **Literature/Columns** | 5-6 | Metaphors, nuanced perspectives, cultural commentary. | Inferring implicit meaning, analyzing tone. |

## 5. Writing Task Specifications (TOPIK II)

The app must support the four specific tasks of the TOPIK II writing section:

1. **Task 51 (Short Answer/Fill-in)**: Complete a sentence in a given context (email, notice). Focuses on appropriate grammar and formality.
2. **Task 52 (Short Answer/Fill-in)**: Complete a sentence in a descriptive/expository text. Focuses on logical flow and vocabulary.
3. **Task 53 (Short Essay)**: Describe a graph or chart (200-300 characters). Focuses on objective data description without personal opinion.
4. **Task 54 (Long Essay)**: Write a logical essay on a given social topic (600-700 characters). Focuses on structure, argumentation, and advanced vocabulary.
