# 2026 High-Probability Theme Pack: Content Specifications

This document provides the specific content required for the 2026 High-Probability Theme Pack (Issue #541). The CTO should use this data to populate the Supabase database.

## 1. Writing Task 54 Prompts & Model Outlines
Each prompt is designed to align with the 2026 focus on Technology, Environment, and Society.

### Theme: Technology & Ethics
**Prompt 1: AI and Job Displacement**
- **Context**: The rapid integration of Generative AI in professional sectors.
- **Requirement**: 
    1. Discuss the primary reason why AI is replacing human labor.
    2. Analyze the potential negative impact on the social structure.
    3. Suggest a way for humans to coexist with AI in the workplace.
- **Model Answer Key Points (Level 6)**:
    - *Intro*: Mention the transition from simple automation to cognitive automation.
    - *Body 1*: Efficiency and cost reduction (cost-effectiveness) as the driver.
    - *Body 2*: Polarization of the job market; disappearance of entry-level cognitive roles.
    - *Conclusion*: Emphasis on "Human-AI Collaboration" and "Lifelong Learning" (평생 교육).

**Prompt 2: Digital Divide & Social Inclusion**
- **Context**: The gap in digital literacy among elderly populations in a super-aged society.
- **Requirement**:
    1. Explain why the digital divide is accelerating.
    2. Discuss the consequences of digital alienation (디지털 소외) on quality of life.
    3. Propose systemic solutions beyond simple education.
- **Model Answer Key Points (Level 6)**:
    - *Intro*: The paradox of high tech vs. low accessibility.
    - *Body 1*: Rapid shift to "Untact" (비대면) services.
    - *Body 2*: Exclusion from essential services (healthcare, banking) leading to social isolation.
    - *Conclusion*: Design of "Inclusive Technology" (포용적 기술) and government-led infrastructure.

**Prompt 3: Algorithm Bias & Objectivity**
- **Context**: The use of AI algorithms in hiring and legal sentencing.
- **Requirement**:
    1. Discuss the perceived objectivity of AI.
    2. Analyze the risk of "encoded bias" (알고리즘 편향).
    3. Argue whether final decisions should always be made by humans.
- **Model Answer Key Points (Level 6)**:
    - *Intro*: The trend of data-driven decision making.
    - *Body 1*: The illusion of neutrality in mathematics.
    - *Body 2*: Garbage In, Garbage Out; reflection of existing social prejudices.
    - *Conclusion*: The necessity of "Human-in-the-loop" systems for ethical accountability.

### Theme: Environment & Sustainability
**Prompt 4: Circular Economy vs. Recycling**
- **Context**: The shift from simple recycling to a zero-waste circular economy.
- **Requirement**:
    1. Define the difference between recycling and a circular economy.
    2. Discuss the challenges of implementing zero-waste in urban areas.
    3. Suggest ways to encourage sustainable consumption.
- **Model Answer Key Points (Level 6)**:
    - *Intro*: Re-evaluating the linear "Take-Make-Waste" model.
    - *Body 1*: Recycling as end-of-pipe vs. Circularity as design-from-start.
    - *Body 2*: Infrastructure limits and the "Convenience Culture" (편의주의 문화).
    - *Conclusion*: Shift to "Slow Consumption" and legislative mandates for producers.

**Prompt 5: Climate Justice & Global Responsibility**
- **Context**: The disparity between developed and developing nations in climate impact.
- **Requirement**:
    1. Explain the concept of climate justice.
    2. Analyze why developed nations bear more responsibility.
    3. Discuss the role of international cooperation in solving the crisis.
- **Model Answer Key Points (Level 6)**:
    - *Intro*: Climate change as an unequal burden.
    - *Body 1*: Historical emissions of industrialized nations.
    - *Body 2*: Vulnerability of developing nations despite low emissions.
    - *Conclusion*: Technology transfer and financial support as a moral imperative.

**Prompt 6: Urban Greenery & Psychological Health**
- **Context**: The increasing need for "Forest Cities" in densely populated metropolises.
- **Requirement**:
    1. Discuss the ecological benefits of urban forests.
    2. Analyze the link between nature and mental health in urban settings.
    3. Suggest a sustainable model for urban planning.
- **Model Answer Key Points (Level 6)**:
    - *Intro*: The alienation of modern urbanites from nature.
    - *Body 1*: Heat island effect mitigation and biodiversity.
    - *Body 2*: Reduction of stress and "Nature Deficit Disorder".
    - *Conclusion*: Integration of "Biophilic Design" into city architecture.

### Theme: Society & Culture
**Prompt 7: Super-Aged Society & Retirement Age**
- **Context**: The economic pressure of a shrinking workforce and aging population.
- **Requirement**:
    1. Discuss the necessity of extending the legal retirement age.
    2. Analyze the potential conflict between different generations (intergenerational conflict).
    3. Propose a balanced approach to labor market restructuring.
- **Model Answer Key Points (Level 6)**:
    - *Intro*: The demographic crisis of the super-aged society.
    - *Body 1*: Utilization of experienced human capital and social security stability.
    - *Body 2*: Youth unemployment and the "bottleneck" effect in promotions.
    - *Conclusion*: Flexible work arrangements and "Job Sharing" (일자리 나누기).

**Prompt 8: Multiculturalism & Social Integration**
- **Context**: The transition of Korea into a multicultural society.
- **Requirement**:
    1. Discuss the benefits of a diverse society.
    2. Analyze the barriers to true social integration.
    3. Suggest a shift from "Assimilation" to "Coexistence".
- **Model Answer Key Points (Level 6)**:
    - *Intro*: The inevitability of multiculturalism due to demographic shifts.
    - *Body 1*: Cultural synergy and global competitiveness.
    - *Body 2*: Deep-seated ethnocentrism and structural discrimination.
    - *Conclusion*: Education on "Cultural Relativity" and legal protections for minorities.

**Prompt 9: Work-Life Balance (Worabel) & Productivity**
- **Context**: The shift in values from "Hard Work" to "Quality of Life".
- **Requirement**:
    1. Analyze the reasons behind the pursuit of work-life balance.
    2. Discuss whether reduced working hours actually lead to higher productivity.
    3. Suggest a corporate culture that supports both growth and rest.
- **Model Answer Key Points (Level 6)**:
    - *Intro*: The collapse of the "Growth-at-all-costs" paradigm.
    - *Body 1*: Burnout syndrome and the desire for self-actualization.
    - *Body 2*: The "Efficiency Paradox"; output vs. hours spent.
    - *Conclusion*: Results-oriented evaluation instead of presence-oriented evaluation.

### Theme: Education & Learning
**Prompt 10: AI in Education vs. Human Teachers**
- **Context**: The rise of personalized AI tutors.
- **Requirement**:
    1. Discuss the advantages of AI-driven personalized learning.
    2. Analyze the irreplaceable role of the human teacher.
    3. Predict the future form of the classroom.
- **Model Answer Key Points (Level 6)**:
    - *Intro*: The disruption of traditional one-size-fits-all education.
    - *Body 1*: Hyper-personalization and efficiency in knowledge transfer.
    - *Body 2*: Emotional support, ethical guidance, and social mediation.
    - *Conclusion*: The "Hybrid Model" where AI handles content and humans handle mentorship.
