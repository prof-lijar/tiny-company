# v2.3 Beta Feedback Instruments: Validating the Intelligence Layer

## 1. Objective
The goal of the v2.3 Beta is to validate that the "Intelligence Layer" provides measurable value beyond simple debugging. We are specifically testing for the four "Aha! Moments" defined in the onboarding experience.

## 2. Beta Success Metrics (Exit Criteria for v2.4)
The transition to v2.4 (Enterprise Scale-Up) will be triggered when the following criteria are met:

| Metric | Target | Validation Method |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Aha! Moment Reach** | $\ge 70\%$ of Beta users experience at least 2 of the 4 Aha! moments. | User Survey / Telemetry |
| **Time-to-Fix Reduction** | $\ge 30\%$ reduction in time spent fixing reasoning bugs via Pattern Vault. | User Interviews / Self-Reporting |
| **Token ROI Validation** | $\ge 50\%$ of users report a "measurable" reduction in token cost after Pruning. | Pruning Report Logs |
| **Confidence Lift** | $\ge 80\%$ of users report "Higher Confidence" in production readiness after Adversarial Synthesis. | Post-Beta Survey |
| **Feature Retention** | $\ge 40\%$ of users use the IDE Extension daily. | Telemetry |

---

## 3. Quantitative Survey (The "Value Pulse")
*To be sent to all Beta participants at the midpoint and end of the Beta.*

### Section A: Feature Utility (Likert Scale: 1-Strongly Disagree $\rightarrow$ 5-Strongly Agree)
1. **Pattern Vault:** "The suggested fixes from the Pattern Vault were relevant and saved me significant manual effort."
2. **IDE Integration:** "The Live-Link (Trace $\rightarrow$ Prompt) significantly reduced the cognitive load of debugging."
3. **Cognitive Pruning:** "The Efficiency Audit provided clear, actionable insights into where my agent was wasting tokens."
4. **Adversarial Synthesis:** "The Fragility Report identified edge cases I would have otherwise missed before production."

### Section B: The "Aha!" Check (Binary: Yes/No)
1. Did you encounter a situation where a "Proven Fix" from the vault solved a problem you were struggling with?
2. Did the VS Code extension make you feel like the "wall" between the trace and the code disappeared?
3. Did you successfully reduce your token usage based on a Pruning suggestion?
4. Did you fix a critical bug discovered by the Adversarial Generator?

---

## 4. Qualitative Interview Script (The "Deep Dive")
*To be conducted with 5-10 "Power Users" and 5 "Churned/Inactive" users.*

### Introduction
"We want to understand not just *if* the tools work, but *how* they change your mental model of building agents. We're looking for the 'magic' and the 'friction'."

### Theme 1: The Pattern Vault (Collective Intelligence)
- "Walk me through the first time you applied a fix from the Vault. What was the problem, and how did it feel when the solution appeared?"
- "If the Vault suggested something that didn't work, what happened? How did you react?"

### Theme 2: The IDE Experience (Contextual Clarity)
- "How has your workflow changed now that you have the Side-by-Side view and Live-Link? Do you still use the CLI for primary debugging?"
- "What is the one thing missing from the IDE integration that would make it indispensable?"

### Theme 3: Efficiency & Cost (Invisible ROI)
- "When you saw your first Pruning Report, what was your internal reaction? Did the 'Tokens Saved' metric feel real or theoretical?"
- "Did you feel hesitant to prune your prompts? Why or why not?"

### Theme 4: Hardening (The Bulletproof Moment)
- "Describe the most 'surprising' bug the Adversarial Generator found. How did that change your perspective on the stability of your agent?"
- "Do you trust the `.tw-test` gates? Why or why not?"

### Conclusion
- "If you had to choose only one of these features to keep, which one would it be and why?"
- "What is the 'missing link' between v2.3 and a tool you would pay for as an enterprise customer?"
