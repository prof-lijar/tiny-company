# v2.2 Growth and Revenue Model: Cost-per-Correction Engine

## 1. Executive Summary
The v2.2 "Reasoning IDE" marks a strategic shift from a diagnostic tool to a corrective platform. The primary growth lever is the **"Correction Preview" Beta**, designed to demonstrate the immediate value of the "Fix-It" (Meta-Prompt) engine. 

By allowing users to experience a limited number of high-value corrections for free, we create a high-intent conversion pipeline to the Pro and Enterprise tiers. This model leverages a **Product-Led Growth (PLG)** strategy where the product's core utility (the "Aha! moment" of a one-click fix) drives acquisition and conversion, minimizing reliance on paid marketing.

---

## 2. Growth Model: The Beta-to-Paid Pipeline

### 2.1 The "Correction Preview" Funnel
The Beta serves as the primary acquisition channel. We utilize a "Freemium-to-Value" bridge:

1. **Acquisition**: Users join the Beta via the waitlist or community outreach.
2. **Activation (The Aha! Moment)**: User identifies a reasoning loop $\rightarrow$ clicks "Fix-It" $\rightarrow$ sees a working prompt correction.
3. **Engagement**: User performs multiple corrections across different prompts, establishing the tool as essential.
4. **Conversion Trigger**: Once the user reaches the **Beta Correction Limit** (e.g., 10 corrections), they are prompted to upgrade to **Pro** for higher limits or **Enterprise** for team-wide deployment.

### 2.2 Conversion Assumptions
Based on the "Correction Preview" value proposition, we estimate the following conversion rates:

| Stage | Conversion Rate | Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Beta User $\rightarrow$ Active User** | 60% | Users who perform at least 1 correction |
| **Active User $\rightarrow$ Pro Tier** | 15% | Power users/Individual developers |
| **Active User $\rightarrow$ Enterprise** | 5% | Team leads/Company-wide adoption |
| **Total Beta $\rightarrow$ Paid** | **20%** | Aggregate conversion of active beta users |

## 4. Unit Economics (LTV & CAC)

### 4.1 Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)
The "Correction Preview" Beta significantly reduces CAC by converting the product itself into the primary marketing channel.

- **Organic CAC**: $0.00 (Driven by GitHub, community, and Beta referrals).
- **Blended CAC**: ~$120.00 (Including small spends on targeted AI newsletters).
- **Max Allowable CAC**: $195.00 (To maintain a 3x LTV/CAC ratio).

### 4.2 Lifetime Value (LTV)
Calculated for the **Pro Tier**:
- **Monthly Contribution Margin**: $39.00 - $1.73 (COGS) = $37.27
- **Churn Rate**: 5% (Avg. lifetime 20 months)
- **LTV**: $37.27 $\times$ 20 = **$745.40**

### 4.3 LTV/CAC Ratio
- **Blended Ratio**: $745.40 / $120 = **6.21x**
- **Conclusion**: The unit economics are extremely healthy. The high margin on the "Fix-It" engine allows for aggressive organic growth without risking profitability.

## 5. Financial Risks & Sensitivity Analysis

### 5.1 Token Cost Volatility
If frontier model costs increase by 5x, the cost per correction rises to ~$0.17.
- **Impact**: Monthly COGS for a Pro user increases from $1.73 to $8.50.
- **Result**: Contribution margin remains high (~78%), meaning the model is resilient to pricing shifts in the LLM market.

### 5.2 Conversion Rate Sensitivity
If the Beta $\rightarrow$ Paid conversion rate drops from 20% to 5%:
- **Impact**: Revenue in Month 12 drops from $1.13M to ~$283k.
- **Result**: The company remains profitable due to low fixed OpEx, but growth targets would need to be revised.

### 5.3 Infrastructure Scaling
While the architecture is local-first, the "Fix-It" API is a centralized bottleneck.
- **Risk**: API latency or downtime during peak Beta usage.
- **Mitigation**: Implementation of request queuing and multi-model routing (routing simple fixes to cheaper/faster models) to maintain UX and cost efficiency.
