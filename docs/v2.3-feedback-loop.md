# v2.3 Beta Feedback Loop & Exit Criteria

This document outlines the strategy for capturing and synthesizing feedback during the v2.3 "Intelligence Layer" Beta phase to ensure the product meets the needs of power users and enterprises before General Availability (GA).

## 1. Quantitative Metrics (The "What")

We will track the following KPIs to measure the actual impact of the v2.3 features.

### 1.1 Feature Adoption & Usage
- **Pattern Vault Utilization:** % of successful fixes that were suggested by the Pattern Vault vs. manually derived.
- **CRI Integration Rate:** % of Beta users who have integrated `tw verify-all` into their CI/CD pipeline.
- **Pruning Efficiency:** Average reduction in token count per trace after applying Cognitive Pruning suggestions.
- **IDE Plugin Stickiness:** Daily Active Users (DAU) of the VS Code extension vs. the Web UI.

### 1.2 Performance & Quality
- **Time to Resolution (TTR):** Average time from "Failure Detected" to "Verified Fix" (target: 40% reduction vs v2.2).
- **Regression Rate:** % of prompts that break existing reasoning paths after a "fix" is applied (target: < 5%).
- **Adversarial Discovery Rate:** Number of critical edge cases found by the Adversarial Synthesis tool before they were hit in production.

## 2. Qualitative Feedback (The "Why")

Quantitative data tells us *that* something is happening; qualitative data tells us *why*.

### 2.1 Feedback Channels
- **In-App "Quick-Feedback":** A one-click "Was this pruning suggestion helpful?" (Yes/No) on every pruning action.
- **Weekly Beta Pulse:** A short, 3-question survey sent every Friday:
    1. What was the most frustrating part of your workflow this week?
    2. Which v2.3 feature saved you the most time?
    3. On a scale of 1-10, how much do you trust the Pattern Vault's suggestions?
- **Bi-Weekly User Interviews:** 30-minute deep dives with 3-5 power users to observe their "Reasoning Workflow" in the IDE.
- **Beta Discord/Slack Channel:** Real-time monitoring of "friction points" and "aha moments."

### 2.2 Synthesis Process
- **Weekly Feedback Sync:** Product and Engineering review the "Friction Log" (aggregated complaints/bugs).
- **The "Aha!" Map:** Documenting specific scenarios where users experienced a sudden leap in productivity.

## 3. Beta Exit Criteria (The "When")

We will transition from Beta to General Availability (GA) when the following conditions are met:

### 3.1 Technical Stability
- [ ] **Zero P0/P1 Bugs:** No critical crashes in the VS Code extension or CRI runner.
- [ ] **Vault Reliability:** Pattern Vault suggestion accuracy (Precision) > 80%.
- [ ] **CRI Performance:** `tw verify-all` execution time is acceptable for CI/CD (e.g., < 2 mins for medium prompt sets).

### 3.2 Value Validation
- [ ] **TTR Reduction:** At least 50% of Beta users report a perceived reduction in time spent fixing reasoning bugs.
- [ ] **Token ROI:** Demonstrated average token saving of > 15% across the Beta cohort via Pruning.
- [ ] **CRI Adoption:** At least 30% of Beta users have a functioning `.tw-test` suite in their repo.

### 3.3 Operational Readiness
- [ ] **Documentation:** User guide for the Intelligence Layer is complete and verified.
- [ ] **Onboarding:** The "Aha! Moment" for the Pattern Vault occurs within the first 15 minutes of use for new Beta users.
