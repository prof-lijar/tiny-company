# v2.2 Beta Feedback & Analysis Framework

This framework defines how we capture, analyze, and act upon user feedback during the 'Correction Preview' Beta. The goal is to transform raw telemetry and user sentiment into actionable product refinements for the full v2.2 release.

## 1. Feedback Collection Mechanism

We will employ a multi-layered approach to capture feedback, moving from low-friction quantitative data to high-friction qualitative insights.

### A. In-App "Micro-Feedback" (Quantitative)
- **Trigger**: Immediately following a 'Fix-It' activation.
- **UI Element**: A small feedback widget appearing next to the corrected output.
- **Inputs**: 
    - Binary/Ternary status: ✅ Solved | ⚠️ Partial | ❌ Failed.
    - Effort Score: 1-5 scale (How much manual effort was saved?).
- **Goal**: High-volume data to track **Fix-It Success Rate (FSR)** in real-time.

### B. "Deep Dive" Prompts (Qualitative)
- **Trigger**: Triggered only when a user selects ⚠️ Partial or ❌ Failed.
- **UI Element**: A modal popup appearing after the status selection.
- **Inputs**: 
    - Checkboxes for common failure modes (e.g., "Introduced new error", "No change").
    - Text field: "Expected vs. Actual".
- **Goal**: Root cause analysis for the CTO and Engineering team.

### C. Weekly Pulse Surveys (Strategic)
- **Trigger**: Sent via email/notification every Friday to Beta participants.
- **Inputs**: 
    - Net Promoter Score (NPS) specific to the Reasoning IDE.
    - "What is the one thing that still feels like 'toil'?"
- **Goal**: Identify systemic friction and sentiment trends.

## 2. Success Metrics (Summary)

We refer to `docs/v2.2-beta-success-metrics.md` for detailed KPIs. The primary triggers for full release are:
- **FSR (Fix-It Success Rate)**: > 65%.
- **PIR (Prompt Iteration Reduction)**: > 30% reduction in manual edits.
- **$\Delta RE$**: Positive reasoning efficiency delta in 80% of cases.
- **TTR (Time-to-Resolution)**: 50% reduction vs. manual debugging.

## 4. Cohort 1 Analysis Plan

We will segment users to understand if the product is serving different personas differently.

### A. Segmentation Logic
- **Power Users**: Top 10% by 'Fix-It' activation frequency and acceptance rate.
- **Strugglers**: Users with high activation frequency but low acceptance rate (< 30%).
- **Passive Users**: Users who have access but rarely trigger 'Fix-It'.

### B. Analysis Process
1. **Pattern Mining**: Analyze the `telemetry` logs for "Power Users" to identify which types of reasoning failures are being solved most effectively.
2. **Friction Mapping**: Conduct 1:1 interviews with "Strugglers" to determine if the failures are due to model limitations or a misunderstanding of the tool.
3. **Gold Standard Update**: Feed the successful paths from Power Users directly into the `Gold Standard Registry` to improve the baseline for all users.

---

## 5. Onboarding Integration

The feedback loop is not an afterthought; it is integrated into the first-mile experience:

1. **The "Feedback Pact"**: During onboarding, users are explicitly told: *"You are part of the Correction Preview. Your feedback on the 'Fix-It' button directly tunes the engine in real-time."*
2. **Guided First Fix**: The first time a user triggers 'Fix-It', a tooltip explains the feedback widget: *"Did this work? Let us know so we can improve the engine."*
3. **The Feedback Incentive**: High-contributing users (those providing detailed 'Deep Dive' feedback) are granted "early access" to v2.3 features.
