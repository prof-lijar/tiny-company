# v2.2 Revenue Projection Model: The Reasoning IDE

## 1. Executive Summary
This document provides a 12-month revenue projection for TraceWhisper v2.2. Following the transition to a "Reasoning IDE" with a tiered pricing model (Free, Pro, Enterprise), the product is positioned for high gross margins due to its **Local-First architecture**, which offloads the majority of compute and storage costs to the end-user.

While the architecture is local-first, the "Correction Suite" (Fix-It button) introduces a variable COGS component based on LLM token consumption.

### 2.3 Cost Structure
Costs are now split between Fixed OpEx and Variable COGS (Cost of Goods Sold).

#### 2.3.1 Fixed OpEx
Includes hosting, domains, and base administrative costs.
- Scaling from $2,500 (Month 1) to $6,000 (Month 12).

#### 2.3.2 Variable COGS (Correction Suite)
Based on `docs/v2.2-cost-per-correction.md`:
- **Cost per Correction**: ~$0.0345
- **Estimated Usage**: 50 corrections/month per Pro/Enterprise user.
- **Eligible Users**: Pro + Enterprise tiers (30% of MAU in "Expected" scenario).
- **Formula**: $\text{COGS} = \text{MAU} \times \text{Paid\%} \times \text{AvgCorrections} \times \text{CostPerCorrection}$

## 4. Break-Even Analysis

### 4.1 Sunk Cost Recovery
The total investment to be recovered includes the v2.2 R&D costs ($5,000) and the cumulative monthly costs.
- **Break-even**: Achieved in **Month 1** across all scenarios. The high ARPU of the Pro/Enterprise tiers easily covers both fixed OpEx and the variable COGS of the "Fix-It" engine.

### 4.2 Unit Economics (Pro User)
- **Monthly Revenue**: $39.00
- **Monthly COGS (at 50 corrections)**: $1.73
- **Contribution Margin**: **95.5%**

---

## 5. Financial Risks & Sensitivity

### 5.1 Token Cost Volatility
The model is resilient to token cost increases. Even if the cost per correction increases by 10x ($\approx \$0.35$), the monthly COGS for a Pro user would be $\approx \$17.50$, maintaining a positive contribution margin of $\approx 55\%$.

### 5.2 Usage Spikes
If the average number of corrections per user spikes from 50 to 500, the variable COGS would increase by 10x. In the "Expected" scenario at Month 12, this would increase costs by $\approx \$230k$, which is still a small fraction of the projected $\$1.1M$ monthly revenue.

### 5.3 Conclusion
The integration of actual COGS for the "Fix-It" suite confirms the extreme profitability of the Reasoning IDE. The value-based pricing model ($39/$149) creates a massive buffer against the variable costs of frontier LLM calls.
