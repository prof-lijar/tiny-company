## 3. Theoretical Variance Impact Analysis
We have identified three primary "Cost Leaks" in the Variance Report. Here is how they would impact the unit economics if they materialize:

### 3.1 Prompt Bloat (Input Tokens > 4,500)
If input tokens increase by 50% (to ~6,750 tokens) due to larger traces or system prompts:
- **New Unit Cost:** ~$0.0458 (+33%)
- **Pro Monthly COGS:** ~$2.29
- **Impact:** Negligible. Margin remains > 90%.

### 3.2 Verbosity (Output Tokens > 800)
If the model generates 1,200 tokens per fix instead of 800:
- **New Unit Cost:** ~$0.0405 (+17%)
- **Pro Monthly COGS:** ~$2.03
- **Impact:** Negligible.

### 3.3 Retry Loops (Multiple Fix-Its per Error)
If users require an average of 2 corrections to resolve a single issue:
- **Effective Unit Cost:** $0.069 (+100%)
- **Pro Monthly COGS:** ~$3.45
- **Impact:** Low. Even doubling the cost per correction only reduces the Pro margin from 95.5% to ~91%.

## 4. Sustainability Recommendation
**Verdict: HIGHLY SUSTAINABLE**

Based on the current pricing ($39 Pro / $149 Enterprise) and the theoretical cost of the "Fix-It" engine, the monetization strategy is extremely robust. 

Even in a "Worst Case Scenario" where:
1. Prompt Bloat occurs (+33% cost)
2. Verbosity increases (+17% cost)
3. Retry Loops double the usage (+100% cost)
**The total unit cost would rise to ~$0.09, and monthly COGS for a Pro user would be ~$4.50. This still leaves a monthly contribution margin of $34.50 per Pro user.**

The primary risk to sustainability is NOT the cost of tokens, but rather the **Conversion Rate** (Beta $\rightarrow$ Paid). As noted in the Growth Model, a drop in conversion from 20% to 5% has a far more significant impact on the bottom line than token cost volatility.
