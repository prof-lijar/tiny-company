# v2.2 CAC/LTV Projection Model: Reasoning IDE

## 1. Executive Summary
This document analyzes the unit economics for the v2.2 "Reasoning IDE" tier. The goal is to ensure that the cost of acquiring a professional user (CAC) is significantly lower than the lifetime value (LTV) they generate, maintaining a healthy LTV/CAC ratio and a sustainable payback period.

Based on current pricing and bootstrapped growth assumptions, the v2.2 Pro tier exhibits strong unit economics, provided that organic growth remains the primary acquisition driver.

---

## 2. Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) Model

We distinguish between **Organic** (community-driven) and **Paid** (targeted) acquisition channels.

### A. Assumptions
- **High-Intent Lead:** An AI Engineer or Developer actively seeking to optimize agentic workflows.
- **Lead Cost (Paid):** ~$20.00 per lead (via sponsored newsletters, niche AI directories, or targeted ads).
- **Conversion Rate (Lead $\rightarrow$ Pro):** 5% (conservative estimate for high-intent users).
- **Organic Conversion Rate:** 2% (lower intent, higher volume).

### B. CAC Calculations
| Channel | Formula | Estimated CAC |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Organic** | $\frac{\$0}{\text{Conversion Rate}}$ | **$0.00** (Cash cost) |
| **Paid** | $\frac{\$20.00}{0.05}$ | **$400.00** |
| **Blended** | $(70\% \text{ Organic} + 30\% \text{ Paid})$ | **$120.00** |

*Note: Blended CAC assumes a growth mix where the majority of users are acquired through GitHub, word-of-mouth, and open-source visibility.*

---

## 3. Lifetime Value (LTV) Model

LTV is calculated based on the revenue generated by a user before they churn, adjusted for the cost of servicing that user (Gross Margin).

### A. Assumptions (Pro Tier)
- **Monthly Price:** $39.00
- **Monthly Variable Cost (API/Infra):** ~$9.75 (est. 25% of revenue)
- **Gross Margin:** 75% ($\approx \$29.25 / \text{month}$)
- **Monthly Churn Rate:** 5% (industry average for Pro developer tools)
- **Average Customer Lifetime:** $\frac{1}{0.05} = 20 \text{ months}$

### B. LTV Calculation
$$\text{LTV} = \text{Monthly Contribution Margin} \times \text{Average Lifetime}$$
$$\text{LTV} = \$29.25 \times 20 = \mathbf{\$585.00}$$

---

## 4. Unit Economics Analysis

### A. LTV/CAC Ratio
The LTV/CAC ratio is the primary indicator of marketing efficiency. A ratio of $3x$ is generally considered the benchmark for healthy SaaS growth.

| Scenario | LTV / CAC | Status |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Blended** | $\$585 / \$120 = \mathbf{4.87x}$ | ✅ Very Healthy |
| **Paid Only** | $\$585 / \$400 = \mathbf{1.46x}$ | ⚠️ Risky |

### B. Payback Period
The time required to recover the CAC from the monthly contribution margin.

- **Blended Payback:** $\frac{\$120}{\$29.25} \approx \mathbf{4.1 \text{ months}}$
- **Paid Payback:** $\frac{\$400}{\$29.25} \approx \mathbf{13.7 \text{ months}}$

---

## 5. Recommendations & Guardrails

### Maximum Allowable CAC
To maintain a target **LTV/CAC ratio of 3x**, the maximum allowable CAC for the Pro tier is:
$$\text{Max CAC} = \frac{\$585}{3} = \mathbf{\$195.00}$$

### Strategic Recommendations
1. **Prioritize Organic Loops:** Since Paid CAC ($\$400$) exceeds our Max Allowable CAC ($\$195$), we should not rely on paid advertising for the Pro tier. Instead, we should double down on "Product-Led Growth" (PLG) via the Free tier.
2. **Enterprise Upsell:** The LTV of an Enterprise user ($\$149/\text{mo}$) is significantly higher. We should use the Pro tier as a "stepping stone" to Enterprise, where the LTV/CAC ratio will be even more favorable.
3. **Churn Mitigation:** A reduction in churn from 5% to 3% would increase LTV from $\$585$ to $\approx \$975$, drastically improving the viability of paid acquisition.
4. **API Cost Optimization:** As the "Correction Suite" is API-intensive, any reduction in the "cost per fix" directly increases the LTV.
