# Social Media Plan: TraceWhisper 'Correction Preview' Beta

## 1. Platform Strategy

### X (Twitter) - The "Developer Hub"
- **Goal:** Drive high-intent signups for the Correction Preview Beta and showcase the 'Prompt Surgery Gallery'.
- **Tone:** Technical, punchy, focusing on the "Aha!" moment of the Fix-It button.
- **Format:** "Swipe" carousels (Loop $\rightarrow$ Wand $\rightarrow$ Fix), short demo clips, and threads on the "Correction Gap".
- **Cadence:** 3-5x per week.

### LinkedIn - The "Enterprise/Operator Hub"
- **Goal:** Position TraceWhisper as the tool that closes the "Correction Gap" for AI Engineering teams.
- **Tone:** Professional, ROI-driven, focused on reducing Time-to-First-Fix (TTFF).
- **Format:** Case studies from the Surgery Gallery, long-form posts on "Vibe-based Engineering" vs. "Surgical Correction", and product updates.
- **Cadence:** 1-2x per week.

### Reddit (r/LocalLLaMA, r/LangChain) - The "Feedback Loop"
- **Goal:** Gain technical validation and recruit power users for the Beta.
- **Tone:** Transparent, builder-to-builder, focusing on the meta-prompting logic.
- **Format:** Deep dives into specific reasoning failure patterns (e.g., "Tool-Output Denial") and how they are solved.
- **Cadence:** Strategic posts.

## 2. Content Pillars

### Pillar 1: The Correction Gap (The Pain)
- Focus on the void between *detecting* a bug and *deploying* a fix.
- The frustration of "Tweak $\rightarrow$ Run $\rightarrow$ Hope" cycles.
- Messaging: "Stop guessing. Start fixing."

### Pillar 2: The Prompt Surgery Gallery (The Evidence)
- **The "Surgical" Approach:** Showcase the "Before $\rightarrow$ Wand $\rightarrow$ After" flow.
- Use real examples: Infinite Search, Contradictory Constraints, Tool Denial.
- Messaging: "We don't just claim it works; we show you the surgery."

### Pillar 3: The Reasoning IDE (The Future)
- Move from simple observability (Diagnostic) to a full optimization environment (IDE).
- Focus on the "Fix-It" button as the core catalyst for speed.
- Messaging: "Close the loop between observability and engineering."
