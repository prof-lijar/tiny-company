# TraceWhisper Privacy Policy

**Effective Date:** May 21, 2026
**Version:** 2.4.0 (Enterprise Edition)

TraceWhisper is committed to protecting the privacy and security of our users' and our customers' data. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, and safeguard information within the TraceWhisper platform, specifically addressing the requirements of the v2.4 Enterprise Scale-Up.

## 1. Data Collection

### 1.1 User Account Data
We collect basic identity information (name, email, organization) required for account creation and authentication. For Enterprise customers, this is typically handled via third-party Identity Providers (IdP) such as Okta, Azure AD, or Google Workspace via SAML/OIDC.

### 1.2 Intellectual Property & Reasoning Data
The core of TraceWhisper involves processing:
- **Reasoning Traces:** The cognitive paths generated by AI agents.
- **Prompts:** The input configurations used to drive agent behavior.
- **Reasoning Patterns:** The "fixes" and "Golden Paths" stored in the Vaults.

### 1.3 Telemetry and Usage Data
We collect anonymized telemetry data to improve platform performance, such as API latency, error rates, and feature usage patterns.

## 2. Data Usage and Processing

### 2.1 Purpose of Processing
Data is processed solely for the purpose of providing the TraceWhisper service, including:
- Facilitating the sharing of reasoning patterns via Vaults.
- Performing Autonomous Prompt Optimization (APO).
- Generating compliance reports and audit logs.
- Verifying adherence to "Golden Paths."

### 2.2 No Model Training
**TraceWhisper does not use Customer reasoning patterns, prompts, or traces to train its own global models.** All data remains the property of the Customer and is used only for the Customer's specific instance of the service.

## 3. Enterprise Data Handling & Compliance

### 3.1 Multi-tenancy and Isolation
We employ strict logical isolation at the organizational level. Data from one Enterprise Customer is cryptographically and logically separated from all other customers.

### 3.2 Data Residency
Enterprise Customers may specify the geographic region where their Vault data is stored to meet local regulatory requirements (e.g., EU-only storage for GDPR compliance).

### 3.3 Security Measures
To ensure the integrity and confidentiality of data, we implement:
- **Encryption at Rest:** All data in Vaults is encrypted using AES-256.
- **Encryption in Transit:** All communication is secured via TLS 1.3.
- **SOC2 Compliance:** Our infrastructure and internal processes are designed to meet SOC2 Type II standards for security, availability, and confidentiality.

## 4. GDPR and Regulatory Compliance

### 4.1 Data Controller vs. Processor
In the context of Enterprise deployments, the **Customer is the Data Controller**, and **TraceWhisper is the Data Processor**. We process personal data only on the documented instructions of the Customer.

### 4.2 User Rights
Under GDPR and similar regulations, users may request:
- **Access:** To see what personal data is being processed.
- **Rectification:** To correct inaccurate data.
- **Erasure:** To have their data deleted (subject to the Customer's retention policies).
- **Portability:** To export their data in a structured format.

## 5. Data Retention and Deletion
Data is retained for as long as the Enterprise subscription is active. Upon termination of service, TraceWhisper provides a grace period for the Customer to export all Vault data, after which all organizational data is permanently deleted from our systems.

## 6. Third-Party Integrations
TraceWhisper integrates with third-party Git providers (GitHub Enterprise, GitLab Self-Managed) and IdPs. Data exchanged with these providers is limited to what is necessary for authentication and the "Reasoning Peer Review" (Trace-Diff) functionality.
